# Homework 9: Personal Ethics Statement
Chapter 5 of Ethical Machines pointed out something I hadn't really thought about before. Companies love throwing around words like "fairness" and "accountability" in their AI ethics statements, but those words are so vague they mean basically nothing. Blackman makes the point that even the KKK would say they value fairness, which is a wild way to put it, but it works. If everyone agrees with your values, your values probably aren't saying anything useful.
The big idea that clicked for me was thinking about your "ethical nightmares" first. Instead of starting with a list of buzzwords, start with the worst thing your AI could do to someone and work backwards. That way your values are actually tied to something real and specific, instead of just sounding good on paper.
